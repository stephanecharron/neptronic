Products Recommended for Application
One of the most important factors in the preservation of art collections is the stability of the surrounding environment requiring temperature and humidity to be strictly controlled. Fluctuations in the humidity generate mechanical stress in hygroscopic materials. This stress is caused by the expansion and contraction of the fibers as the ambient humidity swings up and down.
Many priceless manuscripts, books, works of art and other exhibited objects have been destroyed or damaged because they were not kept in properly controlled atmosphere.
Special museum exhibits often come with strict humidity and temperature specifications which must be met for them to go on display.
In general relative humidity maintained uniformly at 40-50% RH in storage rooms, vaults and galleries prolongs the life of valuable collections. Some materials and artefacts must be segregated as their environment needs differ. Metals should be kept below 35% RH to prevent corrosion.
Paintings: rapid humidity fluctuations damage the canvas as well as the paint layers. Each individual layer reacts to moisture loss in a different way causing them to blister and the paint to flake off.
Paper and papyrus: although moisture can be put back into these materials once they have dried out, a constant hydrating and dehydrating cycle is not good for the paper structure and can cause damage.
