Operation

This configuration consists of multiple vertical dispersion channels mounted on a single horizontal header.

  • Low pressure steam from an in-house boiler enters the SKD steam supply line.
  • Any condensate flows through an F&T steam trap and returns to the boiler.
  • Using the temperature sensor signal, the controller determines if the steam is hot enough and that the steam supply is free of condensate.
  • Upon a demand for humidity, the SKDESC controller slowly opens the isolating steam valve feeding steam to the distributor’s heat exchanger on the horizontal header.
  • The steam flows through a steam separator to separate steam and condensate. Any condensate flows through an F&T steam trap and returns to the boiler.
  • Using another temperature sensor signal, the controller determines if the steam in the heat exchanger supply line is hot enough and that it’s free of condensate.
  • The SKDESC controller slowly opens the steam modulating valve feeding steam to the distributor’s horizontal header dispersion channel. Any condensate falls on the heat exchanger and is re-evaporated.
  • The steam escapes the dispersion system through multiple engineered eyelets on the vertical tubes and mixes with the airflow to maintain the desired humidity level.

The SKD steam injection system is the ultimate solution for applications where clean low pressure steam is available, such as:

Overview

The objective of the Neptronic patent-pending pressurized condensate return (PCR) system is to return condensate generated within the steam dispersion tubes of a direct steam humidifier back to the boiler.


This innovative, patent-pending Neptronic technology is developed to simplify installation, provide maintenance-free and energy efficient operation, and to enable monitoring of the system for direct steam applications.

Features

PCR = Quality
  • Trusted Neptronic peripherals: PCR tank, actuators, sensors and controller are developed and manufactured by Neptronic
  • No flooding of header
  • All components are located outside the duct for easy access
PCR = Maintenance Free
  • Real-time status and monitoring
  • No systematic verification required
  • No de-scaling required
  • No downtime
PCR = No Loss of Energy
  • No gravity drain; condensate is sent back to the boiler
  • All components are low voltage
  • Only 0.013 lb/hr of steam to push 10 lb/hr of condensate

Operation

Humidifier Process

During the humidification process steam fills the header, and then travels up the steam distributors and through steam ejector eyelets. Condensate generated from heat transfer within the vertical tubes accumulates at the bottom of the header.


PCR Process
  • The header's integrated slope forces accumulated condensate to the drain port (1), which then flows into the PCR tank (2)
  • When the tank level setpoint is attained, the ESC controller opens the Steam supply valve (3) to empty the PCR tank (2) into the Condensate return (5) system.
  • The check valve (4) prevents steam pressure from reversing into the header.
  • The ESC controller continues to monitor the tank's condensate level and temperature.
  • When the PCR tank (2) is empty, the ESC controller closes the Steam supply valve (3) and opens the Relief valve(6) to partially relieve pressure in the PCR tank (2) and allow the flow of condensate from the header to the PCR tank (2).
  • With the Relief valve (6) open, the check valve (7) prevents backflow from the Condensate return (5) to the PCR tank (2).
